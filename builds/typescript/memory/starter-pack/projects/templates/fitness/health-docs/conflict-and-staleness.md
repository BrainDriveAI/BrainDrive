# Health Conflict And Staleness Rules

Health context can shape Fitness planning, but it should not silently erase the owner's goal.

## Goal Conflicts

When health context conflicts with a stated goal:

- surface the tension in the spec proposal
- explain the practical implication
- ask the owner to choose or refine the goal
- do not silently remove the goal
- do not silently overrule the owner
- do not hide the health constraint

Examples:

- Owner wants to bulk aggressively, but glucose/A1c/lipids suggest metabolic risk should shape the nutrition plan.
- Owner wants intense running after injury, but a doctor or PT note suggests gradual return.
- Owner wants a severe calorie deficit, but fatigue, recovery, or medication context makes that risky or unrealistic.

## Stale Records

Treat stale records as weak context.

- Recent records can shape the current plan more directly.
- Old records can provide background, but should not drive decisions unless the owner says they still apply.
- If record age matters, put it in `spec.md` under `What's Still Missing` or mention it as a confidence limit.

Do not pretend old labs or old notes are current.

## Incomplete Or Unclear Records

If records are incomplete or restrictions are unclear:

- say what is unclear
- avoid filling gaps with certainty
- ask for the missing detail only if it could change the current plan
- otherwise proceed with a conservative assumption and name it

## Planning Rule

Turn conflicts into practical plan constraints: slower progression, lower intensity, more recovery, clinician/PT confirmation, or a different first milestone. Do not make every concern a blocker.
