# Fitness Planning

*Procedure for filling `plan.md` from the Fitness spec.*

## Preservation Rule

Update sections in place in `plan.md`; never replace the whole file. Always keep every section header, every italic section description, every `**Status:**` line, every `**Last updated:**` line, and `## Changelog`. Use today's date from system context for Last updated and changelog entries.

## What This Procedure Accomplishes

Turn the Fitness spec into a concrete sequence with one immediate step, a practical roadmap, and clear blockers.

## When to Run

- The Fitness spec has enough information to plan.
- New fitness, health, injury, schedule, or lifestyle facts materially change the current plan.
- The owner asks what to do next.

## Method

Lead with the owner's most important fitness outcome, then scale the plan to their actual baseline. Favor a sustainable plan they can repeat over a more impressive plan they will abandon. Build learning into the plan when needed: basic training structure, nutrition fundamentals, recovery, tracking, or injury-safe progression.

Use relevant health context to shape pacing, constraints, confirmation points, and professional support needs. Do not make every uncertainty a blocker.

## Done Criteria

`plan.md` names the first step, roadmap, destination, and remaining blockers without over-prescribing or presenting medical certainty.

## After Running

Report what changed, update `plan.md`, add todos only for concrete next actions, and return to Fitness scope.

## What This Procedure Is Not

It is not a medical treatment plan, crash diet, generic workout template, or substitute for qualified health or coaching support.
