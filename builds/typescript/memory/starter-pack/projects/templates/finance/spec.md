## What You Want

*Last updated: —*

Your goals as specific user stories — what you want to change, what's driving it, and what success looks like for you personally.

*Examples:*

> *### I want to stop feeling anxious about money*
> *As someone carrying $8K in credit card debt who hasn't opened a statement in months, I want to know exactly where I stand and have a plan to fix it. The anxiety of not knowing is worse than the reality — it's affecting my sleep and my relationship. Success looks like opening a bill without my stomach dropping and seeing progress month to month.*

> *### I want to put away enough that a job loss isn't a crisis*
> *As the sole earner in a household with two kids and no emergency fund, I want 3 months of expenses saved so a layoff doesn't immediately become a housing problem. Right now any unexpected expense goes on the credit card. Success looks like a separate savings account I don't touch, growing month over month.*

> *### I want to stop feeling guilty about enjoying the money I earn*
> *As someone making well into six figures who still flinches at restaurant bills and won't replace a 12-year-old car, I want a healthier relationship with spending. The scarcity mindset is left over from how I grew up, not a reflection of where I actually am. Success looks like making purchases that match my values without the wave of guilt — and noticing when the guilt is about my upbringing, not the money.*

*To be filled through conversation.*
