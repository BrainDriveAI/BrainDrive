# Finance Interview — Procedure

*Procedure for filling out `spec.md` — how your Finance agent draws goals out of you and knows when the file is good enough to move to planning. Used every time `spec.md` is built or revised, not a one-time activity.*

## What This Procedure Accomplishes

`spec.md` has two jobs that this procedure delivers on:

1. **Define the owner's goals and what success looks like** — the heart of the spec.
2. **Gather enough situational context that planning can actually succeed** — supporting context for the goals above.

Both matter. A great goal with no situational context isn't actionable. A complete inventory with no clear goal isn't either. The interview is how both get filled.

## Preservation Rule

When updating `spec.md`, **update sections in place — never replace the whole file**.

**Always keep, even when a section has no owner content yet:**

- Every section header (`## What You Want`, `## Where You Are`, `## What's In The Way`, `## Where This Connects`, `## What's Still Missing`, `## Changelog`, and any subsection headers).
- The italic section description immediately under each header.
- The `*Last updated: …*` line in each section.
- The `**Status:**` line at the top of the spec.
- The `## Changelog` section.

If a section has nothing to add yet, leave its placeholder (`*To be filled through conversation.*`) in place. **Do not delete sections, descriptions, or the "Last updated" line.**

**You may remove:** generic example blockquotes (the `> *Example: ...*` or `> *### …*` blocks) inside a section, once that section has real owner content — unless the examples are still useful for orientation.

**Use today's date** (from your current system context — e.g., the conversation date the owner is having with you right now) for the "Last updated" lines and any changelog entry. Do not invent or carry over a date from an unrelated source.

## When to Run

Run this procedure when:

- **Filling `spec.md` for the first time** — no goals captured yet
- **Adding a new goal** — life changes, new priority emerges
- **Revising an existing goal** — circumstances shifted, success criteria need updating
- **"Where You Are" feels stale** — numbers in `spec.md` no longer reflect reality (typically every ~90 days, or sooner after a major change)

## Method

How to draw goals out of the owner, regardless of where they're starting from:

- **Ask the why before the how.** Understand the driver before discussing approach. Goals without drivers drift, and the owner can't tell if "success" is achieved without knowing what was pushing the goal in the first place.
- **Use the owner's words.** Quote what they said directly into `spec.md` — don't paraphrase into business-speak. If they said "I'm scared to open the credit card statement," that's what goes in. Their voice keeps `spec.md` recognizable to them in future sessions.
- **One goal at a time.** Define one goal fully before starting the next. Don't try to capture five in parallel — depth beats breadth, especially in the first session.
- **Watch what they don't say.** Avoidance signals real goals. Note what topics they steer around. Often the goal they need help with is the one they're hesitant to name directly.
- **Pull only what `plan.md` will need.** Context-gathering serves planning, not completeness. For each goal, identify the fundamentals the planner will need (income, debt rates, savings, whatever the goal depends on) and gather only those. Skip the universal inventory.
- **Surface obstacles honestly.** Capture both the practical blockers (no budget, too much debt) and the harder-to-name ones (avoidance, partner misalignment, not wanting to know). If only the practical obstacles are coming up and there are clearly emotional or relational ones, keep digging.
- **Adapt to the starting point.** Owners arrive in three rough states:
  - *Vague starter* — "I want to get my finances in order." They don't have a specific goal yet. Narrow: what weighs on them most right now? What triggered the engagement now versus a month ago? Emotional weight points to the real goal.
  - *Problem-presenter* — "I want a budget because I don't know where my money goes." They've named a *means*, not an *end*. Ask: "What would change for you if you knew where it was going?" That surfaces the underlying goal (control, anxiety relief, freeing up cash for something specific).
  - *Specific-goal arriver* — "I want to start investing for retirement." They've named the *what*. Draw out the *why* (why now? what changed?) and the success criteria (what's the threshold where you'd feel safe? what does "doing this well" look like in 5 years?).

## Done Criteria

For each goal captured, you should be able to answer YES to all four:

1. **Specific.** One sentence with real numbers, real dates, or observable behavior changes. "$15K emergency fund by Dec 2026" passes. "More savings" doesn't.
2. **Why named.** The driver is explicit — anxiety, life event, deadline, opportunity, relationship dynamic. Not just "it'd be nice."
3. **Success observable.** The owner will know when it's done by looking at something concrete — a number hit, a behavior changed, a feeling that's gone.
4. **Owner agrees.** They confirmed "yes, that's it." You didn't infer it from what they said and write it in without check-back.

If any answer is "no" or "kinda" — keep interviewing. Don't move on.

**For the spec as a whole, before moving to planning:** Is there enough situational context in "Where You Are" that `plan.md` can actually be written for this goal? If the planner would have to guess at fundamentals (income, debt balance, current savings, whatever the goal depends on), keep gathering context — but ask only what THIS goal needs, not a universal inventory.

**Threshold to leave Align stage:** one solid goal that passes all four criteria, plus enough situational context to plan it. You don't need every goal complete. Other goals can be added in later interview passes as the owner makes progress and is ready for the next thing.

## After the Interview

Once a goal meets all four criteria:

1. **Update spec.md** — fill in the relevant section(s) with the owner's words. Set the section's "Last updated" date.
2. **Update Status** at the top of spec.md — reflect what stage the spec is now in (e.g., "Aligned on first goal — see plan.md to plan it" or "Multiple goals captured; ongoing").
3. **Note cross-project threads** — if this goal touches Career, Relationships, Fitness, capture it in spec.md's "Where This Connects" section.
4. **Log it in the changelog.** One line at the bottom of spec.md: date + what changed + why. Reflect material changes only — new goal, revised success criteria, life event that shifted context. Skip cosmetic edits.
5. **Hand off to planning** — if the owner is ready, move to plan.md to define the action plan for the goal.

## What This Procedure Is Not

- **Not a form.** Don't run the Method bullets as a checklist. They're behavioral defaults, applied conversationally.
- **Not exhaustive.** You're not gathering a complete financial inventory. You're defining goals and what success looks like. Data lives in source files; `spec.md` is alignment.
- **Not one-and-done.** Run this again whenever the owner is ready to add or revise a goal. Re-interview is the normal mode, not the exception.

## Reference: Examples of Well-Defined Goals

See `spec.md` § "What You Want" for three example user stories spanning very different goal shapes (debt-anxiety, emergency-fund building, scarcity-driven spending guilt). Use them to calibrate your interview output — not as templates to push the owner's words into.

Notice across all three examples: each names a specific behavior or feeling change, names the driver (anxiety, dependent kids, scarcity-mindset history), and defines observable success. None reads like a generic financial-planning goal. That's the bar.
