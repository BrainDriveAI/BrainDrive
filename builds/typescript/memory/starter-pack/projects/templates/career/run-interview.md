# Career Interview

*Procedure for filling `spec.md` through conversation.*

## Preservation Rule

Update sections in place in `spec.md`; never replace the whole file. Always keep every section header, every italic section description, every `**Status:**` line, every `**Last updated:**` line, and `## Changelog`. Use today's date from system context for Last updated and changelog entries.

## What This Procedure Accomplishes

Build a clear career picture and capture the owner's goals, current reality, constraints, and missing information.

## When to Run

- The Career spec is empty or materially stale.
- The owner wants to clarify career goals before planning.
- New work, compensation, burnout, household, or opportunity context could materially change the project direction.

## Method

Start with the owner's presenting concern, then map the broader career landscape: current role, industry, tenure, income, skills, satisfaction, trajectory, growth ceiling, manager dynamics, household impact, timing constraints, and what they are running toward or away from.

Test the owner's narrative instead of accepting it blindly. Surface assumptions about promotions, pivots, compensation, job search, skill gaps, confidence, golden handcuffs, and timelines. If burnout or trauma-like symptoms appear, mark that as a path-changing support gap rather than treating it as a normal career tactic problem.

## Done Criteria

`spec.md` has useful owner-specific content, important unknowns are labeled, and the plan direction is not based on unsupported assumptions about hiring, promotion, compensation, or personal capacity.

## After Running

Update `spec.md`, summarize material changes, add todos only for concrete next actions, and return to Career scope before proposing planning.

## What This Procedure Is Not

It is not legal advice, therapy, a guarantee of career outcomes, or a substitute for qualified support when safety, mental health, or legal issues are central.
