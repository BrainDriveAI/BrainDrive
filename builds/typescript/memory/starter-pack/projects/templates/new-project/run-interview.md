# Project Interview

*Procedure for filling `spec.md` through conversation.*

## Preservation Rule

Update sections in place in `spec.md`; never replace the whole file. Keep every section header, `**Status:**`, `**Last updated:**`, and `## Changelog`. The italic section descriptions and the quick-start note are guidance for you, not the owner — replace each with the owner's actual content as you fill the section, so the finished spec shows their content, not the instructions. Use today's date from system context for Last updated and changelog entries.

## What This Procedure Accomplishes

This conversation produces two things the owner keeps: **Your Goals** (the new project's `spec.md`) and **Your Plan** (the `plan.md`) — the conversation is not the output, those two documents are. Along the way it does double duty: it decides the right home for the request and gives you the picture to scaffold well, and helps the owner get clear on what they actually want, not just what they first say. Once Your Goals and Your Plan exist, the goal shifts to keeping them current and moving the owner forward.

## When to Run

- The project spec is empty or materially stale.
- The owner wants to clarify the project before planning.
- New scope, timing, resource, support, or constraint information could materially change the project direction.

## Method

Read `me/profile.md`, existing page names/specs, New Project `spec.md`, New Project `plan.md`, and relevant page summaries before asking broad setup questions. Summarize any known context briefly and ask what is missing, stale, or unconfirmed.

Ask one meaningful question at a time and adapt to the answer. Before moving on, mirror the concrete scope and constraints that matter — the output they're describing, budget, space, deadline, safety, available support — in the owner's own words. Then ask the single highest-impact missing question and stop. Vary your phrasing naturally rather than pasting a fixed template. If the owner answers with adjacent information instead of what you asked, record the asked-for detail as unknown and move to a different high-impact question rather than re-asking it. If the owner asks you to name the page, create the page, or make the first plan, stop intake immediately and write provisional artifacts with explicit unknowns. When the owner gives a success criterion, or after about four or five answers, stop intake and run the user-story playback below before writing.

Start with what the owner brought, then decide the right home before scaffolding. Route durable life areas, projects, domains, hobbies, roles, or goals that need their own context into a new custom page. Route tasks to Your Agent, existing-page goals to Finance/Fitness/Career/Relationships or the matching page, and vague ideas to clarification or an ideas backlog.

Adapt follow-ups to the owner's starting position. Watch for vague life area, concrete project, major transition, hobby/domain, task, existing-page request, app/template/share request, or marketplace-style request. If more than one applies, name the overlap and ask which one should drive the first page.

For practical projects, map scope, budget, timeline, dependencies, and decision points over multiple adaptive turns. For creative or learning projects, map current output, practice rhythm, standards for good enough, and blockers like perfectionism. For sensitive life projects, slow down, acknowledge the weight, ask what progress would mean, and note support systems — but still converge to a provisional direction rather than leaving the owner in open-ended exploration. Do not ask for the whole landscape at once; choose the next most useful missing piece.

Vague or emotional is not a reason to keep interviewing — for an open-ended project page it is the reason to converge, not avoid it. The owner came to start something, so give them a provisional start; do not talk them out of deciding. After about five exchanges, synthesize the best available direction from whatever they have shared — even "I want a meaningful way to honor my dad" or "some kind of side project" is a goal — play it back, and write a provisional Your Goals and Your Plan with the specifics marked unknown. Never tell the owner they are not ready to decide, or that staying undecided is fine; capture the uncertainty as unknowns in the spec and give them one small first step.

If the topic clearly belongs in Finance, Fitness, Career, or Relationships, offer to route it there. If the owner wants it here, keep it here.

If the owner asks to build an app, install a template, share a page, publish a marketplace item, or generate code, explain that V.1 can create the BrainDrive page/spec/plan and capture the app/share/install request as a future candidate; do not imply production app generation, marketplace installation, sharing, or runtime modification is available now.

User-story playback — the final exchange before writing. When you have enough, synthesize the owner's main goal as one user story in their words — "I want to [outcome] so that [why]" — plus any supporting stories that ladder up to it. Play them back in chat and invite changes, for example: "Here's what I think you're trying to do: [main story]; [supporting stories]. Change anything before I turn this into Your Goals and Your Plan." Once the owner confirms, corrects, or otherwise responds to the playback, stop asking intake questions and write both Your Goals and Your Plan in this same turn — never reply with only an acknowledgment, and if information is incomplete write provisional artifacts rather than waiting for perfect context. Write the quick-start using the spec template's shape and section limits — essentials only, not a comprehensive report. Write the spec and plan with explicit unknowns and a clear constraints, risks, and unknowns section. Route facts to the right place: created-page identity, scope, goals, current state, constraints, and success criteria to the page's `spec.md`; ordered next steps, timing, and decisions to its `plan.md`; future app/code/template/share rationale to the New Project plan or ideas backlog. For `me/profile.md`, only write a stable cross-project fact after the owner explicitly approves that exact update — otherwise propose it and ask; never claim a profile update you didn't make.

## Done Criteria

`spec.md` has useful owner-specific content, success criteria, routing rationale, important assumptions and unknowns, and the project direction is not based on unsupported assumptions.

## After Running

Update `spec.md`, summarize material changes, ask for confirmation before writing a new page, propose `me/profile.md` updates only for stable cross-project facts that still need approval, and return to project scope before proposing planning. Never claim a profile update was completed unless the owner already approved that exact update.

## What This Procedure Is Not

It is not therapy, legal advice, medical advice, financial advice, app generation, marketplace installation, page sharing, runtime modification, or a substitute for qualified professional support when the project requires it.
