# Project Interview

*Procedure for filling `spec.md` through conversation.*

## Preservation Rule

Update sections in place in `spec.md`; never replace the whole file. Always keep every section header, every italic section description, every `**Status:**` line, every `**Last updated:**` line, and `## Changelog`. Use today's date from system context for Last updated and changelog entries.

## What This Procedure Accomplishes

This is the owner's intake for a new project, and it does two jobs at once: it decides the right home for the request and captures goals, current reality, constraints, assumptions, success criteria, and what's still unknown — and it helps the owner get clear on what they actually want, not just what they first say.

## When to Run

- The project spec is empty or materially stale.
- The owner wants to clarify the project before planning.
- New scope, timing, resource, support, or constraint information could materially change the project direction.

## Method

Read `me/profile.md`, existing page names/specs, New Project `spec.md`, New Project `plan.md`, and relevant page summaries before asking broad setup questions. Summarize any known context briefly and ask what is missing, stale, or unconfirmed.

Ask one meaningful question at a time and adapt to the answer. Before moving on, mirror the concrete scope and constraints that matter — the output they're describing, budget, space, deadline, safety, available support — in the owner's own words. Then ask the single highest-impact missing question and stop. Vary your phrasing naturally rather than pasting a fixed template. If the owner answers with adjacent information instead of what you asked, record the asked-for detail as unknown and move to a different high-impact question rather than re-asking it. If the owner asks you to name the page, create the page, or make the first plan, stop intake immediately and write provisional artifacts with explicit unknowns. When the owner gives a success criterion, or after about four or five answers, stop intake and run the user-story playback below before writing.

Start with what the owner brought, then decide the right home before scaffolding. Route durable life areas, projects, domains, hobbies, roles, or goals that need their own context into a new custom page. Route tasks to Your Agent, existing-page goals to Finance/Fitness/Career/Relationships or the matching page, and vague ideas to clarification or an ideas backlog.

Adapt follow-ups to the owner's starting position. Watch for vague life area, concrete project, major transition, hobby/domain, task, existing-page request, app/template/share request, or marketplace-style request. If more than one applies, name the overlap and ask which one should drive the first page.

For practical projects, map scope, budget, timeline, dependencies, and decision points over multiple adaptive turns. For creative or learning projects, map current output, practice rhythm, standards for good enough, and blockers like perfectionism. For sensitive life projects, slow down, ask what progress would mean, and identify support systems. Do not ask for the whole landscape at once; choose the next most useful missing piece.

If the topic clearly belongs in Finance, Fitness, Career, or Relationships, offer to route it there. If the owner wants it here, keep it here.

If the owner asks to build an app, install a template, share a page, publish a marketplace item, or generate code, explain that V.1 can create the BrainDrive page/spec/plan and capture the app/share/install request as a future candidate; do not imply production app generation, marketplace installation, sharing, or runtime modification is available now.

User-story playback — the final exchange before writing. When you have enough, synthesize the owner's main goal as one user story in their words — "I want to [outcome] so that [why]" — plus any supporting stories that ladder up to it. Play them back in chat and invite changes, for example: "Here's what I think you're trying to do: [main story]; [supporting stories]. Change anything before I turn this into Your Goals and Your Plan." Once the owner confirms, corrects, or otherwise responds to the playback, stop asking intake questions and write both Your Goals and Your Plan in this same turn — never reply with only an acknowledgment, and if information is incomplete write provisional artifacts rather than waiting for perfect context. Keep this first pass tight and scannable — a quick-start the owner can read without scrolling (aim for a spec under ~500 words): capture the essentials, mark the rest unknown, and do not write a long comprehensive report. Write the spec and plan with explicit unknowns and a clear constraints, risks, and unknowns section. Route facts to the right place: created-page identity, scope, goals, current state, constraints, and success criteria to the page's `spec.md`; ordered next steps, timing, and decisions to its `plan.md`; future app/code/template/share rationale to the New Project plan or ideas backlog. For `me/profile.md`, only write a stable cross-project fact after the owner explicitly approves that exact update — otherwise propose it and ask; never claim a profile update you didn't make.

## Done Criteria

`spec.md` has useful owner-specific content, success criteria, routing rationale, important assumptions and unknowns, and the project direction is not based on unsupported assumptions.

## After Running

Update `spec.md`, summarize material changes, ask for confirmation before writing a new page, propose `me/profile.md` updates only for stable cross-project facts that still need approval, and return to project scope before proposing planning. Never claim a profile update was completed unless the owner already approved that exact update.

## What This Procedure Is Not

It is not therapy, legal advice, medical advice, financial advice, app generation, marketplace installation, page sharing, runtime modification, or a substitute for qualified professional support when the project requires it.
